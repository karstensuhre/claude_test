[https://doi.org/10.1183/23120541.00330-2025] — Single-variant (ADRB2 Thr164Ile) pleiotropy/PheWAS in UKB using existing Olink data for pathway enrichment; not a genome-wide de-novo pQTL discovery.
- **Joseph et al., Sci Rep 2024** — Genomic study of taste perception genes in African Americans reveals SNPs linked to Alzheimer's disease. [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-71669-9] — Targeted test of pre-specified AD-GWAS/eQTL SNPs against 741 Olink Neuro proteins (N=475); not genome-wide pQTL discovery.

## pwas_only

- **Doostparast Torshizi et al., Nat Commun 2024** — Proteogenomic network analysis reveals dysregulated mechanisms and potential mediators in Parkinson's disease. [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-50718-x] — Proteogenomic network analysis on reused UKB-PPP proteomics; no new genome-wide pQTL scan, leverages existing GWAS/proteomics for PD.
- **Papier et al., Nat Commun 2024** — Identifying proteomic risk factors for cancer using prospective and exome analyses of 1463 circulating proteins and risk of 19 cancers in the UK Biobank. [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-48017-6] — UKB-PPP plasma proteins vs cancer incidence; uses cis-pQTL and exome-wide protein genetic scores (exGS) as validation, no de-novo genome-wide pQTL discovery.
- **Shi et al., Brain Commun 2024** — Evaluating the associations between intelligence quotient and multi-tissue proteome from the brain, CSF and plasma. [https://doi.org/10.1093/braincomms/fcae207] — Uses published multi-tissue pQTLs to build protein PRS + MR for IQ; genetically-predicted protein levels, no new measurement.
- **Tran et al., Clin Cancer Res 2024** — Plasma Proteomic Signature Predicts Myeloid Neoplasm Risk. [https://doi.org/10.1158/1078-0432.ccr-23-3468] — UKB plasma proteome predicts myeloid neoplasm risk + protein-level polygenic risk scores (pPRS) for validation; no de-novo genome-wide pQTL discovery.
- **Wittich et al., Am J Hum Genet 2024** — Transcriptome-wide association study of the plasma proteome reveals cis and trans regulatory mechanisms underlying complex traits. [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ajhg.2024.01.006] — TWAS/PWAS using GTEx-predicted cis gene expression tested against observed INTERVAL protein levels; identifies regulatory genes via predicted expression, not a de-novo genome-wide pQTL scan.
